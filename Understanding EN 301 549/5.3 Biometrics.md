# Understanding 5.3 Biometrics
Status of this document: 

⚠️ Draft mode.

## EN 301 549 v4.1.0 draft
Normative requirement (EN 301 549 v4.1.0 draft)
> “Where ICT uses biometric characteristics, the ICT shall not rely on the use of a biometric characteristic as the only means of user identification or control of the ICT.
> NOTE: Some examples of biometric characteristics are fingerprints, eye retinal patterns, voice, and face.” 


## Scope
This clause applies to ICT systems and services that use biometric characteristics for:

- User identification or authentication (e.g., login, sign-in, account access)
- Control of ICT functionality (e.g., voice commands, gesture-based interaction, biometric unlocking of features or devices)

It applies regardless of:

- Whether biometric use is primary or secondary in the system design
- Whether biometrics are used alone or as part of a multi-factor authentication system
- Whether biometric processing occurs locally on a device or remotely in a service
- Whether biometric methods are optional or enabled by default

This requirement covers both access to ICT systems and interaction with ICT functions once access is granted.

## Normative Requirement
Where ICT uses biometric characteristics, the ICT shall not rely on the use of a biometric characteristic as the only means of user identification or control of the ICT.

**At least one alternative method that does not rely on biometric characteristics shall be provided for all functions that use biometric characteristics for identification or control.**

The alternative method shall:

- Be available without requiring successful biometric enrollment
- Provide access to the same functionality as the biometric method
- Not depend on the availability or success of biometric recognition
- Be usable under the same conditions as the biometric method


## Intent
Not all users are able to provide or reliably use biometric characteristics for identification or interaction with ICT systems. Biometric systems may also fail under environmental, technical, or personal conditions.

This requirement ensures that **biometric characteristics are not the sole means of user identification or control of ICT systems and requires at least one non-biometric alternative method to ensure access when biometric methods are unavailable or unsuitable**.

Biometric systems can create **barriers to access** when:

- Conditions prevent users from providing the required biometric input (e.g., missing fingerprint, facial differences, speech impairment).
- Environmental conditions prevent reliable capture (e.g., low-light conditions, background noise, gloves, masks).
- Technical limitations prevent accurate recognition (e.g., sensor errors or algorithmic bias in biometric recognition systems).
- User-related conditions prevent use of biometric methods (e.g., injury, illness, voice loss, carrying an object, or temporary physical constraints).
- Systemic variability or bias causes exclusion (e.g., demographic variation or bias in facial recognition systems).

### Benefits
This requirement benefits:

- Users with permanent disabilities affecting biometric capture (e.g., limb differences affecting fingerprint recognition, speech differences affecting voice recognition).
- Users with temporary impairments (e.g., injury, surgery, illness, voice loss).
- Users whose biometric characteristics change over time.
- Users in environments where biometric capture is unreliable (e.g., low-light conditions, noise, wet or dry hands, gloves, masks, or carrying objects).
- Users who choose not to use biometric authentication for privacy reasons.


### Examples
If a user has previously enrolled in biometric identification and later experiences issues with those biometric characteristics (e.g., damaged fingerprint or changes in facial appearance affecting recognition), **the user can use a non-biometric method to complete login or control functions**.

- A user who can no longer use fingerprint authentication is able to authenticate using a password or PIN.
- A mobile device that supports facial recognition also supports passcode authentication.
- A voice-based authentication system also supports password-based or multi-factor authentication methods.
- An access control system using iris scanning also supports access cards (badges), PIN-based authentication, or physical tokens (passcodes, secure tokens).


## Evaluation methods
If ICT functionality uses biometric characteristics, verify that:

- A non-biometric alternative method is available for all functions that use biometric characteristics.
- The alternative method does not depend on prior biometric enrollment.
- The alternative provides equivalent functionality to biometric methods.
- The alternative is available under the same conditions as biometric characteristics.


### Testing methods
To validate conformance in practice:

1. Identify all functions that use biometric characteristics.
2. Verify that a non-biometric alternative exists for each function.
3. Verify that the alternative can be used without biometric enrollment.
4. Execute end-to-end task completion using only the non-biometric method.
5. Verify that equivalent functionality is achieved without reliance on biometric success.


### Failures
The following are considered failures:

- The ICT depends solely on biometric characteristics for identification or control.
- No non-biometric alternative method is available.
- The alternative method requires prior biometric enrollment.
- The alternative method is only available after biometric failure.
- The alternative method does not provide equivalent functionality.
- The alternative method is not reliably available or discoverable.


### Passing techniques
The following techniques are sufficient to meet the requirement:

- Providing at least one non-biometric alternative method for all biometric functionality.
- Ensuring the alternative is available independently of biometric enrollment.
- Allowing full task completion using non-biometric methods such as passwords, PINs, tokens, or physical credentials.
- Ensuring equivalent functionality between biometric and non-biometric methods.


## Key Terms
**Biometric authentication:**
The process of verifying a user's identity by comparing biometric characteristics against stored reference data.

**Biometric characteristic:**
A measurable physical or behavioral trait used for identification or verification (e.g., fingerprint, face geometry, iris pattern, voice).

**Biometric enrollment:** The process of capturing, registering, and storing a user’s biometric characteristics for use in subsequent biometric methods for identification or verification.

**Biometric method:** A method of identifying or verifying a user based on biometric characteristics, typically involving automated comparison of captured biometric data with stored reference data.

**Control of ICT:**
The use of input methods to operate or interact with ICT systems and functions.

**Non-biometric alternative:**
An authentication or control method that does not use biometric characteristics, such as PINs, passwords, tokens, or physical credentials.

**User identification:**
The process of verifying or confirming a user's identity before granting access to ICT functionality.
